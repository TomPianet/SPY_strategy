Methodology & research story

This page explains the research logic: inputs, model design, why SPY, and what limitations remain.

Research question

Can a weekly rules-based allocation strategy using a set of macro/market variables produce better risk-adjusted performance than Buy & Hold SPY (including leveraged B&H), when we include realistic transaction costs and financing?

Model & workflow

The pipeline produces weekly signals → converts them to execution/effective weights → runs a daily backtest net of: transaction costs (L1 turnover) and financing costs when leverage is used. Hyperparameters can be tuned efficiently (walk-forward / constrained search), with a tuning agent to automate experiments.

Design choices

  • SPY vs QQQ: SPY is used as the main market benchmark (broad S&P 500 exposure). QQQ tests can be shown as an annex if the strategy doesn’t transfer.
  • Gold vs Silver: gold is used as the defensive commodity leg due to liquidity/benchmark relevance in academic and practitioner use.
  • Realism: costs + financing are included to avoid “paper-only” results.

Limitations & future work

  • Regime shifts and structural breaks can change relationships over time.
  • Signal availability / publication lags can matter; execution assumptions must be consistent.
  • Extensions: more assets, alternative macro variables, robustness tests, and additional transaction cost models.
